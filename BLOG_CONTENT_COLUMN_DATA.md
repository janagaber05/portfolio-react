<p>The relationship between designers and developers is crucial for creating successful digital products. <h2>Clear Communication from the Start</h2>
<p>Communication is the foundation of great collaboration. I make sure to involve developers early in the design process, sharing concepts and gathering feedback before finalizing designs. This early involvement prevents misunderstandings and saves time later.</p>

<h2>Organized Design Files</h2>
<p>I organize my Figma files with developers in mind. Clear naming conventions, organized layers, and detailed annotations help developers understand the design intent quickly. I also create component libraries that developers can reference.</p>

<h2>Design Specifications</h2>
<p>I provide detailed specifications for spacing, typography, colors, and interactions. These specs ensure that the final product matches the design vision. I use Figma's built-in tools and sometimes create separate specification documents.</p>

<h2>Interactive Prototypes</h2>
<p>Interactive prototypes help developers understand not just what the design looks like, but how it should behave. I create prototypes that demonstrate animations, transitions, and user flows clearly.</p>

<h2>Regular Check-ins</h2>
<p>I schedule regular check-ins with the development team to review progress, answer questions, and make adjustments as needed. This ongoing communication ensures we stay aligned throughout the development process.</p>

<h2>Conclusion</h2>
<p>Great collaboration between designers and developers leads to better products. By communicating clearly, organizing files thoughtfully, and maintaining an open dialogue, we create digital experiences that are both beautiful and functional.</p>
